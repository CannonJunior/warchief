# CONTEXT FOR RUNNING PLAYS
Dive: A quick-hitting run up the middle, often between the center and guard or guard and tackle. 

Sweep: A run that goes towards the sideline, with the ball carrier running behind blockers. 

Power: A physical running play that often involves a pulling guard leading the way for the running back, creating a strong point of attack. 

Counter: A misdirection play where the running back and quarterback fake a handoff one way before cutting back the other way. 

Trap: A play where an offensive lineman pulls to block a defender who is crashing the line of scrimmage, opening a lane for the running back. 

Zone: A play (either inside or outside) where offensive linemen block a specific area or "zone," and the running back reads the play and cuts to the best available lane within that zone. 

Pitch: A lateral toss of the ball to a player running outside the quarterback, often seen in option offenses. 

Reverse: A trick play where a player runs laterally across the field behind the line of scrimmage before running back in the opposite direction. 

Quarterback Draw: A run play designed to look like a pass, where the quarterback drops back as if to throw, but then runs the ball up the middle. 

Quarterback Sneak: A run play where the quarterback lines up under center, then runs the ball up the middle immediately after taking the snap.

Blast: A powerful running play, similar to a power play, but often focused on a guard-tackle or off-tackle gap.
