# CONTEXT FOR UNITS FROR BLOCKING ACTIONS

## Run Blocks
Drive Block: A straight-ahead block to drive a defender directly in front of the lineman back off the line of scrimmage.

Down Block: Blocking the first defender to the inside by stepping with the inside foot.

Reach Block: A block where the lineman takes a wide, first step to reach the outside shoulder of the defender and seal them inside.

Double-Team Block: Two offensive linemen work together to block a single defender.

Trap Block: A guard or tackle pulls to the playside, leaving their original spot to kick out the first unblocked defender.

Cut Block: A block where the lineman blocks the defender at or below the knee.

Fold Block: An inside blocker (like a guard) blocks "down," and a lineman behind them folds back to block the defender who has crossed the line of scrimmage. 

Seal Block: A block where the offensive lineman seals the defender away from the play, creating a running lane.

Scoop Block: A block where the offensive lineman blocks a linebacker, not a defensive lineman.

Wedge Block: A block where the offensive line forms a "wedge" to create a strong running lane for the ball carrier. 

## Pass Blocks
Man Protection: Each offensive lineman is assigned to block a specific defender.

Slide Protection: The entire offensive line slides to one side to protect that side of the formation.

Zone Protection: Similar to zone run blocking, the linemen block an area of the field rather than a single defender.

Kick Slide: A combination of footwork and hand placement to counter the defender's rush.

Full Slide: The entire offensive line moves in the same direction to protect a specific side. 

## Combination Blocks
Zone Blocking: Linemen are assigned a zone or an area to block, rather than a specific defender. This often involves multiple linemen blocking a single defender if they are in the same zone.

Gap Schemes: Linemen work together to block a "gap" in the defensive line, often using double-team blocks to create a hole for the running back.
