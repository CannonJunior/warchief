# CONTEXT FOR ACTION ROUTE

## Short routes
Flat: A quick route run toward the sideline, typically covering a few yards.

Slant: A quick, sharp cut at a 45-degree angle across the middle of the field.

Hitch: A route where the receiver runs a short distance, stops abruptly, and turns back toward the quarterback.

Drag: A short, in-breaking route that moves horizontally across the middle of the field. 

## Intermediate routes
Curl: Similar to the comeback, the receiver runs upfield before turning back inside to face the quarterback.

Out: The receiver runs downfield and then cuts sharply toward the sideline at a 90-degree angle.

Dig: A route where the receiver runs upfield before cutting sharply at a 90-degree angle toward the middle of the field.

Comeback: The receiver runs downfield, then turns back toward the sideline. 

## Deep routes
Go (or Fly): A straight vertical route where the receiver uses speed to run deep downfield.

Post: The receiver runs downfield before angling toward the goalposts.

Corner: Similar to the post, but the receiver angles toward the corner pylon of the end zone.

Fade: An outside-breaking route to the corner of the end zone, often used against press coverage. 

## Concepts Routes
Wheel: A route that starts like a flat but then breaks vertically up the sideline.

Seam: A route run up the field between deep defenders, often seen in a slot receiver.
